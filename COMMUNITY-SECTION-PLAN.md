# Community Section Plan

> **Last updated:** 2026-03-29
> **Status:** Strategic direction document for a new top-level nav section. Not yet built.

## Purpose

This document defines the direction for a new `Community` section in the Highlander Today navigation. The section groups content that is about the community itself — its people, its history, and its identity as a place — rather than services the platform provides (marketplace, help wanted) or daily content (Local Life, events).

The Community section answers the question: what kind of place is this, who has lived here, and what does it feel like to be part of it?

## Nav Structure

The intended top-level navigation becomes:

`Home | Local Life | Experiences | Market | Help Wanted | Community | About`

The `Community` dropdown contains:

- **History** — local history articles and community memory
- **Moving To** — information for people considering or new to the area
- **Memoriam** — death notices and memorial pages honoring community members

`About` remains a separate section for institutional/platform content (Mission, Blog, Roadmap). `Community` is about the place and its people; `About` is about the platform itself.

## History

History surfaces local history content — articles, photo essays, oral history, documentary references, and archival material about the communities in the Cambria Heights area.

### Relationship to Local Life

Local Life already has a `history-heritage` category. History content could be authored through the existing Local Life article system and tagged with that category, then surfaced into the Community section as a curated view. Alternatively, History could eventually become its own editorial surface with a dedicated submission flow if the volume and editorial needs diverge from standard Local Life articles.

The recommended near-term approach is to use Local Life as the authoring system and surface `history-heritage` articles into the Community section, avoiding a redundant editorial pipeline. If history content develops its own identity and requirements over time, it can be separated later.

### Connection to Verified Local Memory

The History surface is the natural home for the "Verified Local Memory" concept from the blue-sky brainstorming: durable community knowledge built from approved reporting and trusted contributions. As the memorial archive grows and the article sourcing/citations direction matures, History can become an increasingly structured and authoritative local knowledge base.

## Moving To

Moving To serves two audiences: people considering a move to the Cambria Heights area, and people who have recently arrived.

### For Prospective Residents

This surface should present the community in an honest, welcoming way — not a marketing brochure, but a genuine portrait of what life is like here. Content could include:

- overview of the area (geography, towns, school district, character)
- local economy and employment landscape
- cost of living context
- outdoor recreation and natural surroundings
- community organizations, churches, and civic life
- school and education information
- what residents like about living here

This content is largely editorial and can start as static pages, evolving toward dynamic content as the platform matures.

### For New Residents

New residents need practical onboarding — both into the community and into the platform. Content could include:

- how to get started on Highlander Today
- how the trust system works and why it matters
- local stores, services, and resources
- upcoming events and ways to get involved
- how to connect with neighbors through the platform

This is also a natural home for the auto-generated welcome packages described in `MONETIZATION-PLAN.md`. A real estate agent or the platform itself could offer a downloadable or emailed welcome guide pulling from live platform data: nearby stores, upcoming events, community contacts, and onboarding steps.

### Connection to Real Estate

Moving To is a natural complement to any future real estate listing surface. A property listing gains context when it can link to a living portrait of the community — not just square footage and tax records, but what it actually feels like to live there. The Moving To section provides that context layer.

## Memoriam

Memoriam is the public-facing home for death notices and memorial pages. The full direction is documented in `OBITUARIES-PLAN.md`; this section describes how Memoriam fits into the Community nav structure.

### Section Name

"Memoriam" (or "In Memoriam") is the preferred section name. It is dignified, signals respect, and is distinct from the newspaper "Obituaries" framing. It works as both a nav label and a destination page heading.

### Two Content Tiers

As defined in `OBITUARIES-PLAN.md`, Memoriam contains two content types:

**Death Notices** — minimal, factual, time-sensitive records. Free. These inform the community about a death and service details. They are basic community information that a local platform should provide at no cost, in deliberate contrast to newspapers charging hundreds of dollars for the same service.

**Memorial Pages** — richer, longer-lived pages with biography, photos, video, family history, milestones, and moderated community memories. Memorial pages with media hosting may carry a modest fee tied to storage costs, as described in `MONETIZATION-PLAN.md`.

### Homepage Presence

The homepage should include a small, understated block for recent death notices — names and dates only, linking through to the full notice or memorial page. The design should be the quietest element on the page: muted typography, no bold colors, no attention-grabbing treatment. It should feel like a respectful acknowledgment, not a feature competing for attention with events or marketplace content.

The homepage block is a pointer. The Memoriam section under Community is the destination.

### Archive and History Connection

Over time, the Memoriam archive becomes part of community history. Death notices accumulate into a record of who lived here, and memorial pages preserve their stories. This naturally connects to the History surface and the broader "Verified Local Memory" direction. A memorial page created today may be read as local history decades from now.

## Design Considerations

### Tone

The Community section should feel warmer and more reflective than the transactional sections (marketplace, help wanted). History and Memoriam in particular deal with identity and memory, and the design should respect that weight. Moving To can be more inviting and practical in tone, but should still feel grounded rather than promotional.

### Not Overloaded

Community is intentionally limited to three items at launch. The heading is broad enough that other features could live here in the future — the directory/people listings, civic content, or the creator network programming guide. But the section should not be overloaded early. Each addition should earn its place by being clearly "about this place and its people."

## Sequencing

History can start immediately by surfacing existing `history-heritage` Local Life articles into a Community section view. Moving To can start as static editorial pages. Memoriam depends on the obituary/memorial system described in `OBITUARIES-PLAN.md` and should follow the phased rollout defined there.

The nav structure change (adding Community as a top-level dropdown) can ship as soon as there is enough content in at least one subsection to justify the heading. History articles may already provide that starting point.
