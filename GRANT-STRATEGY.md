# Grant Strategy — Highlander Today

> **Last updated:** 2026-04-04
> **Status:** Strategic direction document. No grant applications have been submitted yet.

## Core Recommendation

Highlander Today should pursue grants, but not as a generic software startup pitch.

The strongest framing is:

- local news and community information infrastructure
- civic participation and trusted community coordination
- Appalachian small-town digital infrastructure
- local history, storytelling, and public memory for narrower humanities-aligned projects

The weakest framing is:

- "social network for towns"
- venture-scale software platform
- broad horizontal marketplace startup
- "everything app" language

The project is most fundable when it is described as accountable, place-based public-interest infrastructure for Cambria Heights / Cambria County first, with expansion to additional communities only after proving local value.

## First Structural Decision

The most important pre-application question is organizational structure.

Most strong grant opportunities for this project will expect one of the following:

1. a standalone `501(c)(3)` nonprofit
2. a fiscally sponsored project under an existing `501(c)(3)`
3. a formal partnership where a nonprofit institution is the applicant and Highlander Today is the implementation partner

If Highlander Today remains purely for-profit, the realistic grant pool becomes much smaller and shifts away from journalism/humanities/public-interest philanthropy toward selective economic-development or contract-style opportunities.

## Recommended Positioning

### Primary narrative

Highlander Today is building local digital infrastructure for Cambria Heights / Cambria County, Pennsylvania: a trusted local information and participation system that combines public-interest reporting, events, local business and organization discovery, help wanted, moderated community identity, and accountability-centered messaging.

The platform exists to answer four recurring local questions:

- what is happening locally
- where can I get something
- who can help me
- how can I participate

### Why this framing works

- it is concrete rather than abstract
- it ties the product directly to community function
- it aligns with journalism, civic information, and regional-development funders
- it avoids sounding like a venture pitch looking for philanthropic subsidy

### Secondary framing lanes

Depending on the funder, Highlander Today can emphasize one of four lanes:

1. `Local news / community information`
   - Local Life reporting
   - event visibility
   - public-trust editorial posture
   - trustworthy local discovery

2. `Civic infrastructure / participation`
   - organizations directory
   - public organization pages
   - help wanted
   - local participation loops

3. `Regional economic resilience`
   - business discovery
   - local commerce visibility
   - workforce/help wanted
   - small-town digital infrastructure

4. `Public memory / humanities`
   - local history
   - community storytelling
   - memorial and obituary direction
   - oral-history and place-based archive possibilities

## Best-Fit Grant Targets

These are the highest-priority targets based on current project scope.

### Tier 1: pursue first

#### Press Forward

This is the strongest mission fit if Highlander Today can apply through a nonprofit structure or fiscal sponsor.

Why it fits:

- local-news and community-information thesis
- support for trusted information ecosystems
- infrastructure framing is stronger here than a single-section newsroom pitch
- Highlander Today combines journalism, directory, events, participation, and trust in a way that matches "community information" better than conventional newsroom software

What to pitch:

- Cambria County local information infrastructure pilot
- trusted community directory plus public-interest reporting
- local participation and organization visibility
- accountable digital replacement for fragmented Facebook-group / flyer-board / low-trust local web patterns

#### Community Foundation for the Alleghenies

This is the strongest practical regional funder because Highlander Today is rooted in Cambria County.

Why it fits:

- direct place alignment
- community-benefit framing is legible
- easier to explain local need and local stewardship
- can fund a specific local pilot rather than the whole platform vision

What to pitch:

- community information access for Cambria County residents
- local institutions and organization visibility
- trusted events and help-wanted coordination
- local history / storytelling pilots if needed for narrower grant categories

#### Lenfest Institute for Journalism

This is a strong relationship-building target, especially if the journalism and creator-network sides mature further.

Why it fits:

- Pennsylvania journalism relevance
- experimental local journalism and creator partnerships are compatible with the project's direction
- Highlander Today has a stronger case if it sharpens its public-interest editorial identity and nonprofit readiness

What to pitch:

- rural/small-town local-news infrastructure
- public-interest local reporting plus durable community utility
- future creator-network and local-distribution possibilities only as supporting context, not the lead claim

### Tier 2: pursue with narrower framing or partnerships

#### Appalachian Regional Commission

This is promising if the pitch is regional-development infrastructure rather than "news."

Why it fits:

- Appalachian geography
- community economic resilience
- business and workforce visibility
- digital infrastructure for under-served small communities

What to pitch:

- local business discovery and organizational visibility
- help-wanted and workforce participation
- digital infrastructure supporting community resilience
- possible county or multi-partner implementation with chambers, nonprofits, or municipalities

#### PA Humanities

This is not the right target for the whole platform, but it is a strong fit for a scoped storytelling/history project.

Why it fits:

- public memory and local storytelling
- community identity and place-based narrative
- local history, oral histories, and memorial/archive work

What to pitch:

- a Cambria community oral-history series
- local history/public-memory publishing
- obituary and memorial archive groundwork
- place-based identity storytelling tied to the platform's public mission

### Grantable slice A: local information infrastructure pilot

Best fit:

- Press Forward
- Community Foundation for the Alleghenies

Scope:

- local reporting support
- events visibility
- organization directory presence
- public-interest local information access
- community engagement and trust-building

### Grantable slice B: Appalachian small-town digital resilience

Best fit:

- ARC

Scope:

- business discovery
- help wanted / workforce visibility

- durable local digital infrastructure for community coordination

### Grantable slice C: local history and public memory

Best fit:

- PA Humanities

Scope:

- oral histories

- obituary/memorial archive work

## Immediate Priorities

The recommended order is:

1. Decide whether Highlander Today will create a nonprofit entity, secure fiscal sponsorship, or use a nonprofit partner model.
2. Produce a short grant-ready narrative deck with the project problem, mission, geography, audience, and current live product state.
3. Define a first-year measurable pilot scope for Cambria Heights / Cambria County.
4. Assemble baseline operating data and proof points.
5. Build target-specific outreach and application materials.

## What Grant Readiness Requires

Before serious outreach, Highlander Today should have the following ready.

### Organizational readiness

- clear legal/applicant structure
- one-paragraph explanation of editorial independence
- basic budget or pilot budget
- named leadership/stewardship contacts
- concise explanation of community governance and moderation posture

### Narrative readiness

- a 100-word description
- a one-page overview
- a short statement of community need in Cambria County
- a clear explanation of why this is different from Facebook groups, Patch, or generic classifieds
- a concise explanation of the trust model and why it matters

### Evidence readiness

- screenshots of live product surfaces
- current feature inventory
- community adoption metrics once available
- examples of public-interest use cases the platform already supports

## Metrics To Start Tracking

Even before fundraising, Highlander Today should begin tracking grant-useful metrics.

- monthly active users by community
- number of approved articles published
- number of events submitted and published
- number of directory listings by type
- number of organizations with completed profiles
- number of help-wanted posts
- number of trusted users
- number of vouches completed
- number of moderation actions
- number of messages initiated from directory, help-wanted, or organization surfaces

These are not just product metrics. They are proof that Highlander Today is functioning as community infrastructure.

## Recommended Materials To Produce

Create these before applications begin:

1. `Grant one-pager`
   - mission
   - community served
   - problem
   - current product state
   - requested support

2. `Pilot budget`
   - editorial/community operations
   - product/infrastructure
   - outreach/training
   - contractor or implementation costs

3. `Grant deck`
   - 8-12 slides
   - screenshots
   - community problem statement
   - product thesis
   - pilot plan
   - budget and outcomes

4. `Applicant structure memo`
   - who applies
   - how funds are received
   - who owns the IP/platform
   - how editorial independence is protected

## Risks To Avoid

- applying too early without a nonprofit path
- pitching the entire long-term roadmap instead of a narrow first-year pilot
- sounding like philanthropy is being asked to subsidize a private venture
- emphasizing future multi-tenant expansion before proving local community value
- overloading applications with feature lists instead of community outcomes
- mixing too many narratives in one proposal

## Recommended Near-Term Path

The most pragmatic near-term sequence is:

1. choose a nonprofit, fiscal-sponsorship, or nonprofit-partner structure
2. prepare a Cambria-focused one-page pilot narrative
3. open relationship conversations with:
   - Community Foundation for the Alleghenies
   - Press Forward
   - Lenfest
4. shape a separate narrower humanities concept for PA Humanities
5. revisit ARC once the economic-resilience framing and partnership structure are stronger

## Current Conclusion

Highlander Today is plausibly grantworthy because it is not merely publishing content or building software. It is assembling local community infrastructure across information, trust, participation, and discovery.

The strongest first move is not "apply everywhere." It is to make the project grant-compatible structurally, tighten the Cambria County pilot narrative, and pursue a small number of high-fit funders with tailored framing.
